Echoes

The Literary and Art Magazine


Echoes is South's official literary and art magazine, which offers a compilation of works from the student body in an annual publication produced at the end of the year. It has been around for most of South's history, having been founded in 1974 and is still active to this day. Each year, members of the club review and critique student submissions and vote on which pieces will be present in the magazine. The club also offers various art and writing workshops for its members, directed by the Art and Literary Editors. The issues of the magazine can be found in the library, the art rooms, and various language art rooms for all to enjoy. The magazine comes at no charge and is based on a theme that combines the variety of art and literary works accepted.

When: Thursdays from 3 to 4 in the art room (302).

How to join: Come in during any one of the meetings.

Advantages:

  • Free and relaxed scheduling (you don't have to come every week).
  • Submissions can be from in and out of the club.
  • Pieces that are accepted become part of an official publication.
  • Offers workshops to improve art and literary skills.
  • Is one of the more unique clubs to join at South (offers a variety among other extracurriculars).

Leadership Positions:

  • Technical Editor
  • Public Relations
  • Art Editor
  • Literary Editor
  • Head Editor
